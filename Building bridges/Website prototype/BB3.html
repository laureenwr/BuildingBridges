🤝 Mentoring Programme

The Mentoring & Empowerment Programme

Structured, one-on-one mentoring between girls and FLINTA* of Colour who want to enter the psychosocial field, and professionals of colour who have walked this path before them.

Who is it for?

Two roles, one shared mission

The programme brings together mentees on their way into academic careers, and mentors who are already there — and want to make the path easier for those who follow.

👩🏽
For Mentees
Girls & FLINTA* of Colour · From grade 10
You are in school or early university, interested in psychology, social work, counselling, or related fields. You are looking for guidance, representation, and a community.
👩🏾
For Mentors
Professionals · Researchers · PhD Students
You work in the psychosocial field and have a migration or marginalisation background. You want to give back — one committed meeting per month.

Why Stories Matter

The mirror effect

Research on representation shows that young people from underrepresented groups need to see someone who looks like them succeed in the field they are considering. Without storytelling, this is a research project website. With it, it becomes a mirror.

"Yes. This is for you. Someone who looks like you has been here before you. And she left the light on."

🔬 About the Project

Building Bridges — the research project

A 36-month interdisciplinary project, funded by BMBF and ESF+, across three partner institutions, addressing structural barriers facing girls and FLINTA* of Colour in German higher education and the psychosocial field.

Project Goals

Six core objectives

Developed through participatory research with the communities the project serves.

🎯
Develop the MEP Programme
Build a needs-based, intersectionality-sensitive mentoring and empowerment programme for BIPoC girls and FLINTA*.
🎓
Promote Academic Careers
Support pathways into academic careers in the psychosocial field for participants of colour.
✨
Create Role Models
Integrate mentors of colour to make successful academic biographies visible and inspiring.
💻
Build a Digital Platform
Create a participatory platform for context-sensitive storytelling and peer-to-peer exchange.
🔍
Research Barriers
Investigate discrimination experiences and conditions for increased participation in academic settings.
💪
Strengthen Resilience
Build resilience and unlock potential by identifying and activating individual talents and resources.
Timeline

Three phases, 36 months

September 2024 – August 2027

Phase 1 — Development
Sep 2024 – Feb 2025
Programme design, partner onboarding, first cohort recruitment, foundational research setup, and digital platform architecture.
Phase 2 — Implementation
Mar 2025 – Aug 2026
We are here
Active mentoring cohorts, full workshop programme, participant research activities, and iterative platform development based on community feedback.
Phase 3 — Evaluation & Transfer
Sep 2026 – Aug 2027
Full programme evaluation, publication of research findings, knowledge transfer, and development of a sustainable long-term model.
Structure

Three subprojects — one mission

Each partner institution leads one Teilprojekt (TP), contributing distinct expertise to the shared goal.

TP1 · Scientific Coordination
Research & Evaluation
Freie Universität Berlin
